Executive Summary

Roomie is an app designed to communicate and coordinate with roommates, to increase accountability and track chore progress.

Project Overview

This project was a 3 week sprint, comprised of one week of user research, one week of design, and one week of preparation and presentation. Our main task for this project was to build out the onboarding process and 1-2 working features within the app.

Role and Responsibility

  • User Researcher
  • Wireframe Designer

Deliverables

  • Proto/User Persona
  • Work Flow
  • User Flow
  • Prioritization Matrix
  • Affinity Diagram
  • Lo-Fi to Hi-Fi Wireframes
  • Clickable Prototype

Tools Used

  • Miro
  • Figma
  • Adobe Suite

  • Google Drive
  • Canva

Phase One: User Research

We began our research by conducting five user interviews, leading to the development of our user persona, Jake Maddox. After completing our user persona, we developed our affinity diagram

What's the Problem

As someone non-confrontational, Jake finds it hard to relay his feelings, when he and his roommates find themselves conflicted over who's turn it is to do certain chores or pay for supplies. This causes tension amond roommates, making it hard for Jake to relax.
How might we build an app to assist roommates communicate and organize tasks, responsibilities, and communal resources?
Context of constraints: Inability to use app if they don't have the iOS we develop, some roommates may work longer hours or not have as much free time as others.

Phase Two: Definition and Synthesis

From the data collected in our user research, we then developed an "I like, I wish, what if?" model, allowing us to create a prioritization matrix, a user insight statement, and a defined problem statement. Furthermore, we conducted a competitive analysis to determine what works and what doesn't work from both indirect and direct competitors.

Phase Three: Ideation

Upon completion of our competitive analysis, each team member did a lo-fi sketch of what we envisioned the app to look like and how it would function. After we completed our sketches, we melded the three designs togeth, finding similarities, then adding the individual strenghts of each design.

Phase Four: Prototyping

From the sketches, we developed digital wireframes and began prototyping in invision, linking pages together, and flushing out any glaring mistakes. This was the easiest step out of the entire process, as we had all gotten into our groove.

Phase Five: User Testing

From our user testing, we discovered that there needed to be a separation of components within the chore assignment screen. After the findings from our last round of research, I began harnessing elements of iOS interfacing and flushing out the mid-fidelity prototype.

Phase Six: Conclusion

This project had a pretty quick turnaround, not allowing for much of what we would like to see in this app. In future iterations, we'd like to implement more apartment management features and design an android counterpart to go along with our iOS design.
